# Outbound Mechanics

## Email Jobs

Choose one job before writing:

- reply: get a short answer
- routing: get forwarded to the right owner
- call: earn a short discovery call
- demo: earn permission to show the product
- pilot: earn permission to send or discuss a pilot plan
- revive: restart a silent thread

The email should do that job only.

## Strong First Email Shapes

### Trigger -> Business Consequence -> Soft Ask

Use when there is a real prospect signal.

```text
Subject: team bandwidth

Hi Sarah,

Saw you're hiring across customer success and implementation.

That kind of growth usually creates a second-order problem: managers spend more time keeping teams aligned, focused, and recovered after peak weeks.

[Product] helps [company type] give employees practical support without adding manual work for People teams.

Worth sending over a short pilot plan?
```

### Reframe -> Mechanism -> Ask

Use when personalization data is weak.

```text
Subject: quick thought

Hi Sarah,

In teams where output depends heavily on focus and communication, overload rarely shows up as one clean HR problem.

It shows up as slower follow-through, more manager intervention, and harder recovery after intense cycles.

[Product] helps teams address that with [mechanism], while giving the company [business view] without exposing personal data.

Open to a 15-minute look?
```

### Executive Version

For CEOs/founders, avoid HR-heavy wording. Connect to execution, leadership bandwidth, team stability, speed, and decision quality.

```text
Subject: team load

Hi Alex,

For growing teams, employee support often becomes a leadership bandwidth issue before it becomes a formal People program.

When people take longer to recover after peak work, it shows up in delivery, decision quality, and how much managers have to compensate.

[Product] gives the team a practical self-help layer, with only aggregated visibility for the company.

Would it be useful if I sent a short 3-month pilot plan?
```

## Subject Lines

Use short, plain, internal-looking subjects:

- team load
- quick thought
- pilot idea
- people ops
- team bandwidth
- burnout signal
- follow-up

Avoid emojis, urgency tricks, fake `Re:` / `Fwd:`, the prospect's first name, and product-pitch subjects.

## CTA Bank

Use one CTA:

- "Worth sending over a short pilot plan?"
- "Open to a 15-minute look?"
- "Should I send this to someone else on your team?"
- "Would this sit with you or your People lead?"
- "Useful if I shared a 2-page rollout plan?"
- "Is this worth a quick look, or not a priority right now?"

Avoid:

- "When are you free for 30 minutes?"
- "Can I put time on your calendar?"
- "Are you struggling with burnout right now?"
- "Let me know if you're interested."

## Follow-Up Sequence

Use 3-5 touches:

1. First email: strongest reason and one soft CTA.
2. Follow-up 1: new buyer angle.
3. Follow-up 2: useful asset, pilot plan, benchmark, or diagnostic lens.
4. Follow-up 3: routing or disqualification.
5. Breakup: respectful close with a useful final option.

Avoid "just checking in" unless it is paired with a new reason to respond.

## Links

Ask whether to include a link and which one. If included, make it secondary. Cold first touches usually work better with zero or one link.
