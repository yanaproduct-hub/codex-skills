# Quality Checklist

Run this before giving the final email.

## Reply Likelihood

- Is there one clear job for the email?
- Would the buyer understand why this matters in the first 3-5 seconds?
- Is the opening about the prospect's world, not the sender?
- Does the email avoid depending on one guessed pain?
- Is the CTA easy to answer in one line?
- Is there only one CTA?

## Native English Voice

- Does it sound like a real person wrote it?
- Is it concise without becoming robotic?
- Does it avoid "I hope this email finds you well"?
- Does it avoid "I came across your profile"?
- Does it avoid sales jargon and over-polished phrasing?
- Does it avoid sounding translated from another language?

## Sales Strength

- Is the business consequence concrete?
- Is the buyer role respected?
- Is there a clear bridge from problem to next step?
- Is proof allowed and not overstated?
- If no proof is available, is the product mechanism credible enough?
- Does the ending make it easy to respond now?

## Sequence Quality

- Does each follow-up add a new angle?
- Can each follow-up stand alone?
- Is there a routing email if the recipient is not the owner?
- Is the breakup respectful and useful?

## Risk Check

- No fake urgency.
- No fake `Re:` or `Fwd:`.
- No manipulative guilt.
- No guaranteed sales, reply, medical, legal, financial, or clinical outcomes.
- No banned project terms.
- No unapproved links or proof points.
